Hello! Welcome back to PIE Dev Studios. I'm Phillip, the lead visual development artist for Absolute Momentum. ![Phillip Drawing](assets/phillipk_phillip_portrait.png)

My job is to explore and develop ideas for characters, color palettes, environments, and the overall aesthetic for the project.

Today I’m going to be going over my character design process for our characters in Absolute Momentum.

# Getting Started
When our team first proposed this racing game, the first idea that came to mind was something retro-futuristic and upbeat.

I decided to focus on a grind-fiction like aesthetic for our characters, which is a blend of urban street culture, 
Shibuya Punk, and Y2K futurism.

Games like Jet Set Radio, Splatoon, Persona, and Hi-Fi Rush all use the grind fiction aesthetic.

This style also allows our team to create art assets that don’t require crazy AAA graphics but still hold a bright and 
optimistic feel to compliment the speedy nature of the game.

# Character Guidelines
Before designing the characters, I also had to account for technical limitations of having to use similar base models 
while still making sure they felt like separate people. 

To help me with this, I followed these self imposed guidelines during the design process:
- A signature color,
- Athleisure/Allusion to a physical activity
- Keeping it within a Sci-fi space
- Embracing their culture
- An animal that represents their personality
- And a speed trail

The last 5 especially helped when designing our characters because within Absolute Momentum, these were global racers 
celebrating their culture and environment by racing in a retro-futuristic Olympic style race.

# Designing the Characters
I started off by doing quick sketches for four of our characters: Baku, Etoile, Sangeo, and Lei Lei.

## Baku 
![Baku](assets/GameScreenshots/AbsoluteMomentum/absmom_baku_char_art.png)
Baku is a failing DJ that conducts rave performances through the combination of break dancing and lion dancing. 
His outfit is inspired by lion dancers and rave culture, representing the color red and the Pixiu, 
mythical Chinese creatures that protect the souls of the dead. Coming from a Chinese Filipino background, 
his upbeat and silly personality motivates him to join the race in hopes to find something that he’s good 
at and to make his family proud. 

## Etoile
![Etoile](assets/GameScreenshots/AbsoluteMomentum/absmom_etoile_char_art.png)
Etoile, the racing prodigy, comes from a Vietnamese Creole family who are very supportive of her racing career. 
Her social inept and unintentional bluntness leaves a lot of her personal history a mystery. Representing the 
eel and the racing through the course in a rich blue, her outfit takes inspiration from retro futurism. Despite 
her success in the Absolute Momentum, she wants to pursue astronomy but doesn’t want to disappoint her parents. 

## Sangeo
![Sangeo](assets/GameScreenshots/AbsoluteMomentum/absmom_sangeo_char_art.png)
Sangeo is an curious, unhinged lifeguard who was given an opportunity to race in the Absolute Momentum when their 
country’s representative got injured from the race. Their outfit is based on lifeguard uniforms and the surf punk 
aesthetic, racing boldly with a bright cyan streak and representing a Thresher Shark. They’re excited to participate 
in the race and try something on their own.

## Lei Lei
![Lei Lei](assets/GameScreenshots/AbsoluteMomentum/absmom_leilei_char_art.png)
Lei Lei is a retired multi-winning champion of the Absolute Momentum. Wise, wild, and unserious, she uses the money 
from her wins to support her community and charity. Now working at a shabby cafe, she lives a low maintenance but happy 
life. Representing a panda, her green streaks embody her mascot outfit as she reappears at the current Momentum Rally 
to mentor her grandson Baku.

## Bia 
![Bia](assets/GameScreenshots/AbsoluteMomentum/absmom_bia_char_art.png)
Bia is a proud Grenadian Carniva/Jab Jab Dancer and singer who loves pastries. Her shy, stubborn, ambitious personality 
resembles one of a butterfly, as she races with yellow streaks and an outfit that takes inspirations from Jab Jab Idols. 
They race to promote their family’s spice and rum cake business and show the world Grenada’s accomplishments as a country 
and more than just a small spice island. 
Bia was our first character designed by a new artist Shiv. I did a few touchups and the final character art but the 
foundation and direction was all built by her.

# Conclusion
Overall, I wanted our characters to capture that feeling of cultural celebration found in games like Street Fighter while 
emphasizing the modern cultural fusion that occurs when diverse communities come together and how we can bond and progress 
through art, especially through movement.

I hope this was an insightful dive into our character design process!
